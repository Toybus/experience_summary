# Output Contracts

## Author Experience Asset

默认读者是做同类工作、但业务不同的人。每条经验用以下三个互相验证的部分；只写证据支持的内容：

1. **作者最根本的想法**：用作者会说的话，写出“我为什么这样看”，而不只重复结论。
2. **它怎样影响当前材料里的真实判断**：指出材料中的具体情境、判断或取舍、所看证据和后果，说明第一部分怎样落到了这一次工作上。
3. **同类工作、不同业务的读者怎样使用，以及不能照搬什么**：给出一个能执行的提问、动作或核验方法；再明确写出哪些业务条件必须先确认、哪些原案例细节不能直接搬走。

三部分要能互相核对：第一部分必须能在第二部分找到真实判断的证明；第三部分要保留第一部分的意思，也要承认第二部分的业务边界。若材料只支持其中一部分，不要为了看起来完整而补写其余部分；写明缺少什么和为什么还不能下结论。

每条还要保留：
- 作者表达，以及报告和访谈中的证据出处；
- 成熟度：已验证经验、工作假设或未探索直觉；
- 尚未说清的部分。

语言只能像作者会说的话，或让高中生直接读懂。避免把普通判断堆成抽象术语。

**跨业务自检：** 让一位做同类工作、但业务不同的读者读完后，应该能同时说出作者最深的想法、原案例中证明它的一次真实判断、自己下一步可做的动作，以及不能照搬原案例的条件。例如，分析 DSAT（客户不满意反馈）的人可以是这样的读者，而非唯一读者。

后文 Worked example 只示范经验如何放回分享内容，不是完整的 Author Experience Asset，不能替代上面的三部分契约。

## Prepare the second-layer reader lens

Before rewriting an experience for readers, prepare a compact internal lens for a person doing the same type of work in a different business:

- the comparable task they are trying to do;
- the plausible default judgment or mistake that would make this experience useful;
- the change in judgment the author can help them make;
- the minimum source case, contrast, or artifact needed to make that change credible;
- the condition that limits transfer to another business.

Use this lens to select and explain the experience; do not emit it as another metadata checklist. A second-layer reader shares the problem type, not the source document's business context or exact solution.

## Reader-ready experience

After preserving the Author Experience Asset as the evidence-backed record, rewrite each selected experience as a short, continuous explanation for the second-layer reader. Start from a recognizable problem, tempting shortcut, or decision they may face. State the author's different judgment in the author's voice or plain language, show one source moment where it changed a real conclusion or action, and end with a usable move plus the transfer boundary.

The rewrite changes presentation, not evidence status. Keep uncertainty visible: do not turn a working hypothesis into a proven method, a scenario estimate into a commitment, or one case into a universal sequence. Avoid repeating `author idea`, `source judgment`, and `reader use` as headings; the reader should be able to understand their connection without assembling fields.

Add one **sharing cue** per selected experience in this form: `Start with [reader problem or mistaken instinct], show [one source case/artifact or contrast], and land on [the judgment or action to remember].` This cue is the complete default sharing guidance: do not repeat the experience as a separate sharing outline. It is a speaking aid, not a script, deck outline, or rewritten report.

## Source-Coupled Sharing Content

Expand beyond the sharing cue only when the author asks how to place an experience into a specific source section. Treat the result as placement guidance, not as a new summary document. Follow the source document's reasoning order. For each relevant source section, combine as needed:
1. what original result, table, diagram, or case to show;
2. an author-voice explanation of the result;
3. the experience that shaped this part;
4. a reader-use form suited to that experience.

Place pre-writing or cross-cutting experience briefly at the opening or closing, and point back to the source sections where it operated. Do not emit a separate generic sharing framework.

## Select reader use

| Experience type | Reader-use form |
|---|---|
| Judgment | A question the reader can ask themselves |
| Action | A concrete move to try |
| Evidence | A lightweight verification method |
| Risk | A signal to watch |
| Trade-off | Comparison dimensions |
| Working hypothesis | Something to observe in the reader's own cases |

## Voice check

Prefer words and sentence patterns found in the source and interview. If the author could not naturally say the abstraction aloud, lower it one level.

## Worked example

**Source content:** The report validates whether the Intake channel should exist before designing its automation.

**Author cue:** “有人建议直接把 Chat 放到表单前面，所以我觉得不能先讨论怎么做，应该先回答 Intake 到底做不做。”

**Internal reader lens (preparation only):** A reader designing similar service automation may start by comparing implementation options before checking whether the proposed channel should exist.

**Reader-ready experience:** “做服务自动化时，很容易一上来就比较 Chat、表单或其他实现方案。但如果其中一个建议会改变项目边界，而且判断错了会让后面的设计整体失去意义，我会先验证更高层的问题：Intake 到底要不要做。这次我先验证渠道是否成立，再展开自动化方案。做其他业务时也可以先问，最基础的假设如果不成立，后面的设计是否会整体失去意义；但不能直接照搬这里的渠道结论。”

**Sharing cue:** Start with the instinct to compare automation options, show the decision to validate Intake first, and land on the question that tests whether later design would lose its premise.
